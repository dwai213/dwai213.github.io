Tailbot V1

After Joonbum left for his Assistant Professor position in Korea, I leapfrogged towards another project for my undergraduate research. I worked with Evan Chang Siu to help him build the controller for tailbot, a robot with inertial assisted control by an active tail inspired by lizards.


Because of budgetary and equipment restraints, the controller consisted of various individual breakout components from Sparkfun united together on one a milled, copper PCB. Different from typical boardhouse-fabricated PCBs, copper boards minimized the use of vias and discouraged use of ground planes. Moreover, it made SMD components very inaccessible. As a result of all of these constraints, I was able to design, fabricate, and populate a PCB that became the heart of Tailbot v1, which used the controller for pitch control.

Tailbot became quite a celebrity in the robotics world because it demonstrated simply the insight biologists have gleaned from lizards. It was featured on the front cover of an edition of Nature and made headlines for UC Berkeley's newcenter. I am proud to have Tailbot be one the first robots I contributed to.
